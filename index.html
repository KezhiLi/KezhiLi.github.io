Kenneth (Kezhi) Li is currently a Research Scientist at Imperial College London, Medical Research Council (MRC). He obtained the PhD degree at Imperial College London in Feb, 2013, and B.Eng. degree in Electronic Engineering from and University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), Hefei, China, in 2008, respectively.

He is a key member in the group of behaviour genomics. His research interests are quite broad in several inter-discipline areas, such as seeking for the relation between organism's genotype and phenotype using computer vision and machine learning, statistical signal processing (compressive sensing) and their applications in imaging system and quantum tomography, communication and information theory. Before coming to MRC, he used to be a research associate at University of Cambridge, a research fellow at Royal Institute of Technology (KTH) in Stockholm and a research assistant at Microsoft Research Asia (MSRA) and USTC.
