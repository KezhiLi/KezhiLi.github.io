Kenneth (Kezhi) Li is currently a Research Scientist at Imperial College London. He obtained the PhD degree at Imperial College London in Feb, 2013, and B.Eng. degree in Electronic Engineering from and University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), Hefei, China, in 2008, respectively.

He is a senior research associate in the group of Bio-inspired Technology Centre, Electrical and Electronic Engineering Department, Imperial College. His research interests are quite broad in several inter-discipline areas, such as biomedical signal processing in diabetes management and behaviour genomics using deep neural networksm, data mining and computer vision, structured signal processing/machine learning and their applications in imaging system and quantum tomography, optimization algorithms and information theory. Before coming to Imperial College, he used to be a research scientist at Medical Research Council (MRC), a research associate at University of Cambridge, a research fellow at Royal Institute of Technology (KTH) in Stockholm and a research assistant at Microsoft Research Asia (MSRA) and USTC.
